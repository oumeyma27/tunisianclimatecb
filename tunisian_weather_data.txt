Weather Conditions in Tunisian States during World War II:

1. Tunis:
   - Mild Mediterranean climate with hot summers and mild winters.
   - Occasional rainfall during the winter months.
   - Average temperatures range from 10°C (50°F) in winter to 30°C (86°F) in summer.

2. Sousse:
   - Similar to Tunis, Sousse has a Mediterranean climate with hot summers and mild winters.
   - Average temperatures range from 10°C (50°F) in winter to 30°C (86°F) in summer.
   - Sousse experiences slightly less rainfall compared to Tunis.

3. Bizerte:
   - Located in the northern part of Tunisia, Bizerte also has a Mediterranean climate.
   - Summers are hot and dry, while winters are mild and rainy.
   - Average temperatures range from 8°C (46°F) in winter to 28°C (82°F) in summer.

4. Gafsa:
   - Gafsa has a semi-arid climate with hot summers and mild winters.
   - Summers can be extremely hot with temperatures exceeding 40°C (104°F).
   - Average temperatures range from 10°C (50°F) in winter to 35°C (95°F) in summer.

5. Hello:
   - Located in southwestern Tunisia, Tozeur has a desert climate.
   - Very hot and dry weather with scorching summers and mild winters.
   - Temperatures can reach over 45°C (113°F) during the summer months.

Please note that these weather conditions are based on general climate patterns and may vary slightly from year to year. It's important to consult historical weather data for more accurate information during specific time periods of World War II.
